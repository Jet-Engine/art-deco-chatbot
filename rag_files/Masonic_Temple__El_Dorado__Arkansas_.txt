Masonic Temple (El Dorado, Arkansas)

The Masonic Temple of El Dorado, Arkansas is located at 106-108 North Washington Street, on the west side of the courthouse square.  The four-story masonry building was built in 1923â€“24 to a design by Little Rock architect Charles S. Watts.  It is one of a small number of buildings in Arkansas with Art Deco styling influenced by the Egyptian Revival.  This particular styling was likely influence by the 1922 discovery of the Tomb of Tutankhamun.[2]

The building was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2001,[1] and included in the El Dorado Commercial Historic District in 2003.[3]
